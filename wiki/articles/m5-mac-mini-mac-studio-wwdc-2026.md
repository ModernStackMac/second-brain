# M5 Mac Mini and Mac Studio Leaked for WWDC 2026

> Apple's 2026 Mac lineup leaked — five new models spanning M5 desktops and M6 laptops with OLED, touchscreens, and up to 512GB unified memory.

**Source:** [Forget the M4 Shortage: M5 Mac mini and Mac Studio Leaked for WWDC 2026](https://www.geeky-gadgets.com/2026-new-mac-models/) | **Published:** 2026-04-09 | **Tags:** tech, ai

## Summary

Apple is preparing five new Mac models for 2026, split across two launch windows. The WWDC summer launch includes the Mac Studio (M5 Max/M5 Ultra) and Mac Mini (M5/M5 Pro), while the fall lineup brings a refreshed 24-inch iMac (M5), the new MacBook Ultra (M6 Pro/M6 Max on 2nm), and an updated 14-inch MacBook Pro (M6). The Mac Studio effectively replaces the discontinued Mac Pro as Apple's flagship desktop.

The M5 Ultra tops out at a 36-core CPU, 80-core GPU, and 512GB unified memory — positioning it squarely for professional workloads like 3D rendering, video editing, and AI/ML workflows. The MacBook Ultra introduces several firsts for Apple laptops: OLED display, touchscreen, Dynamic Island, and potentially Face ID.

## Key Takeaways

- **Mac Studio (WWDC 2026):** M5 Max and M5 Ultra chips. M5 Ultra = 36-core CPU, 80-core GPU, up to 512GB unified memory. Replaces Mac Pro.
- **Mac Mini (WWDC 2026):** M5 and M5 Pro chips. M5 Pro = 18-core CPU with ray tracing and enhanced AI capabilities.
- **24-inch iMac (Fall 2026):** M5 chip, design largely unchanged, possible new color options.
- **MacBook Ultra (Fall 2026):** M6 Pro/M6 Max on TSMC 2nm. OLED, touchscreen, Dynamic Island, possibly Face ID. Apple's most expensive laptop.
- **14-inch MacBook Pro (Fall 2026):** M6 chip on 2nm. Performance boost with same design and price point.
- **AI capabilities enhanced across all models** — improved ML, image recognition, and NLP performance baked into the silicon.

## Why It Matters

The M5 Ultra's 512GB unified memory ceiling is significant for local AI model inference and training — relevant as Mac explores more on-device AI workflows with Claude and other models. The Mac Mini M5 Pro with ray tracing and AI acceleration could be a compelling dev machine at a lower price point. Worth watching the WWDC announcement for pricing and actual AI benchmark improvements.
